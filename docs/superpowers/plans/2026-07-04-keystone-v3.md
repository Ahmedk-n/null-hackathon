The concept judge confirmed the owner's suspicion: as shipped, this is **well-executed argument mapping on rails**. The math (product-AND propagation + knockout sensitivity + keyword reweight) is fault-tree analysis in a CAD skin; the core LLM chain is a fixture stub that **discards the judge's decision text**; the context-flips-outcome beat is hand-tuned, not emergent. One judge keystroke ("should we hire a VP of Sales?" → the same microservices graph) reclassifies the project live from "reasoning tool" to "rigged animation."

**The uncopyable combination** (judge's verdict): live extraction + evidence-grounded confidences + a minimum-reinforcement solver, with time-axis stress as the memorable beat:

> "Type your real decision. It grounds every assumption in evidence pulled from your repo and site, finds the one crux that takes everything down, tells you the cheapest set of things to prove before your deadline, and shows the date you fail if you don't."
